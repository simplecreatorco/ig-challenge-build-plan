Step 3 of 6

Your Hook

Your hook is the first frame — the text overlay that stops the scroll. It has one job: make the right person feel like this was made for them.

🎯 Qualifier
Name a specific person in a specific situation. "This is for the [identity] who [specific scenario]."
🔨 Objection Crusher
Lead with the objection as your hook. "Think you need [X] to [outcome]? You don't."
💰 Proof Post
Specific result + relatable identity. "I'm just a [identity] and last [time] I [specific result]."
🪝 Curiosity Gap
Tease the insight, withhold the resolution. "The reason [outcome] isn't happening for you — and it's not what you think."
Three Hook Rules
1. Name a specific person, problem, or number
2. Create a gap between what they know and what they want to know
3. Never be clever when you can be clear
This is the text on screen in the first 1–2 seconds. Keep it punchy — under 15 words is ideal.
Step 4 of 6

Your CTA

Every post needs one specific trigger — the action you want the viewer to take. This is how your post connects to your funnel.

Three CTA Formats That Work
Comment trigger: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you [thing]" — activates ManyChat

DM trigger: "DM me [word] and I'll send you [thing]"

Link trigger: "Link in bio to [specific thing]"
Keep it to one action. The more specific the CTA, the higher the conversion.
